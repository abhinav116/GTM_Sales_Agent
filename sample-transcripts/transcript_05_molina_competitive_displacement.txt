Priya Nair: Good morning everyone. I appreciate you putting three senior people on this call. Before I say anything about RAAPID, I want to understand what is broken. Dr. Osei, do you want to start?

Dr. James Osei: Sure. We have 600,000 Medicare Advantage members across eleven states. We have been using Inovalon for risk adjustment for the last three years. The contract is up in four months. We are actively evaluating whether to renew or replace.

Priya Nair: What has the experience with Inovalon been?

Dr. James Osei: Mixed at best. The retrospective accuracy is acceptable -- they are hitting around 94 percent on our internal audits. But two problems. First, their prospective capability is weak. We are doing prospective coding manually, which is expensive and inconsistent. Second, the RADV audit support has been inadequate. When we got a RADV sample last year, their documentation package was incomplete and our compliance team had to reconstruct a significant amount of the audit trail themselves.

Diane Chu: From an operations perspective, I will add that their customer support is poor. We have had open tickets for integration issues for six months. We have a dedicated account manager but she has limited ability to actually escalate technical problems.

Tom Varghese: And from an IT perspective, Inovalon's API documentation is outdated. We have had to reverse-engineer parts of the integration ourselves. For a vendor of their scale, it has been surprisingly difficult to work with technically.

Priya Nair: So the accuracy is tolerable but the prospective gap, RADV support, and technical partnership are the real issues.

Dr. James Osei: That is a fair summary.

Priya Nair: What does the financial impact of the prospective gap look like?

Diane Chu: Our internal estimate is 6 to 8 percent undercoding on the prospective side. On 600,000 members, our actuaries have put that at 50 to 70 million dollars annually. That is the headline number driving the urgency here.

Priya Nair: That is significant. On RADV support -- what specifically was missing in Inovalon's documentation package?
